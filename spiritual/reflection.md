# Spiritual Guiding Principles

---

## Introduction

As an IT professional and a disciple of Jesus Christ, my technical work is guided by faith, integrity, and a commitment to service. The following principles serve as the foundation for my academic journey and professional career.

---

## Principle 1: Faith in Jesus Christ

* **Why It Matters:** Faith provides purpose, clarity, and direction in both personal life and complex technical problem-solving.
* **Professional Influence:** When encountering complex database bugs or networking challenges, trusting in the Lord gives me patience, perseverance, and wisdom to find effective solutions.
* **Scriptural & Prophetic Alignment:**
  > "Lay not up for yourselves treasures upon earth, where moth and rust doth corrupt, and where thieves break through and steal: But lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven... For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also." — **3 Nephi 13:19–21**
  
  Elder David A. Bednar emphasized that all truth—spiritual and temporal—is unified under Christ:
  > "Gather together in one all things in Christ." — **Elder David A. Bednar (2018)**

---

## Principle 2: Integrity & Ethical Stewardship

* **Why It Matters:** In the technology sector, handling sensitive user data, network security, and database infrastructure requires absolute honesty and accountability.
* **Professional Influence:** I hold myself to high ethical standards when handling system credentials, configuring secure databases, and writing automated scripts.
* **Application in IT:** Adhering to strict cybersecurity standards, respecting data privacy, and prioritizing security best practices in every deployment.

---

## Principle 3: Continuous Learning & Lifelong Service

* **Why It Matters:** Technology evolves rapidly, requiring constant growth, curiosity, and adaptability.
* **Professional Influence:** I view technology as a tool to serve others, solve meaningful real-world problems, and support community growth.
* **Application in Studies:** Actively engaging in peer workshops, sharing technical insights, and creating clear technical documentation to help fellow students and teammates.
