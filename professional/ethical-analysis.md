# Ethical Dilemma Analysis in Information Technology

---

## Executive Summary

As technology professionals, we are frequently entrusted with privileged access to sensitive systems, user data, and organizational infrastructure. This case study analyzes an ethical dilemma regarding data privacy, system authorization, and corporate integrity—applying both industry professional standards and Christian ethical principles.

---

## The Scenario / Situation

During a database migration and cleanup task, a database administrator (DBA) discovers unencrypted personal identifiable information (PII) and access credentials stored in plain text within a legacy production table. Additionally, the DBA notices that several former employees still retain active read/write permissions to this database due to outdated access control lists (ACLs).

---

## Ethical Principles & Standards at Stake

1. **Principle of Integrity & Honesty:** Striving for absolute transparency and honesty in reporting system vulnerabilities rather than ignoring them to avoid project delays.
2. **Stewardship & Accountability:** Recognizing that handling user data requires strict protection against unauthorized access or breaches.
3. **Professional Codes of Ethics (ACM/IEEE):** 
   * Honor confidentiality and privacy.
   * Access computing resources only when authorized or when compelling public good requires it.
   * Know and respect existing laws pertaining to professional work.

---

## Actions Taken & Decision Framework

1. **Immediate Escalation:** Documented the security vulnerability and exposed permissions through formal internal security channels without copying or downloading the exposed PII.
2. **Access Revocation & Remediation:** Worked with system administrators to immediately revoke access for inactive accounts following the **Principle of Least Privilege (PoLP)**.
3. **Data Encryption Standard Implementation:** Recommended implementing column-level encryption and hashing for sensitive data fields in compliance with industry standards.

---

## Personal Reflection & Growth

This experience reinforced that technical competence must always be paired with moral integrity. Faith in Jesus Christ teaches us to act with honesty even when no one is watching. In IT administration, maintaining high ethical standards is essential to building trust, protecting users, and upholding professional honor.
